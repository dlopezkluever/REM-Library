Prometheus

Greek Titan · Fire-Bearer · Culture Hero · Divine Transgressor

ATTESTATION
4 of 5 · 12 sources

Prometheus is a Titan celebrated as the fire-bearer who stole divine flame from the gods and gave it to humanity.1 The act positions him as a liminal figure — simultaneously transgressor and benefactor — a structural role that recurs across cultures with striking consistency.

The research group identifies Prometheus as a central node connecting to Yahweh's withholding of knowledge in Eden, the Hollywood rebel-hero archetype, and Lucifer's identification with light and forbidden illumination.2
